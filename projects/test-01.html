Paranormal at Scale, Discourse in Reddit — Data Cleaning

Data Cleaning · Applied Research · 2026
Python NLP Digital Trace

Overview

This project explores r/Paranormal, a Reddit community centered on first-person reports of anomalous and paranormal activity. The core of this work is the cleaning and processing of raw scraped data into a structured, analysis-ready corpus using spaCy's en_core_web_lg model for lemmatization and linguistic preprocessing.

Context

The subreddit was chosen for its density of self-authored experiential narratives — accounts of haunting, entity contact, and unexplained phenomena written in lay language. The thematic framing draws from Mark Fisher's The Weird and the Eerie (2016), which theorizes two distinct phenomenological registers of the uncanny, and which motivates the analytical questions that the cleaned corpus will be used to investigate in later stages.

Methods

Raw scraped data was cleaned by stripping Markdown, URLs, and Reddit-specific markup. Cleaned text was then lemmatized using spaCy, reducing tokens to their base dictionary forms and removing stopwords and punctuation. Posts below a five-token threshold were filtered out as uninformative. The resulting dataset was written to a parquet file as a fixed-schema artifact consumed by all subsequent notebooks in the pipeline.

Results

The cleaning process reduced the raw corpus dropping deleted or empty submissions, and applying the token filter. The final artifact is approximately 49 MB and retains text at three levels of processing (raw, clean, and lemmatized) alongside temporal metadata. This structured dataset serves as the foundation for all subsequent analyses, enabling reproducibility and consistency across the project pipeline.
